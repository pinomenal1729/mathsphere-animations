What This Means
The point v has not moved — only the description changed. Standard coordinates say "go 3 along e₁, 2 along e₂." The new basis says "go c₁ along b₁, c₂ along b₂." Same destination, different directions.
Key Insight

A basis is a choice of coordinate system. Changing the basis changes how we describe vectors, not what they are. In ML, PCA finds a basis aligned with variance — making patterns visible that were hidden in the standard basis. Learned embeddings find a basis where semantic relationships become geometric.
